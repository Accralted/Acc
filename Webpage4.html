Beginner Friendly Openings You Should Know!

As a beginner, chess may seem like a very complex and huge number openings of which none you can understand. Most beginners will get a losing position by 10 moves due to a bad opening.
Here below are some Beginner Friendly Openings you can play which don't take a huge amount of theory and are easy to play.

1. Italian Game Opening

The Italian Game Opening is a really easy opening comprising of moving the King's Pawn to e4, Knight to f3 and Bishop to c4. It is a very good opening as it facilitates fast development of Kingside pieces and castelling is possible on the next move. The only disadvantage is the bishop can be attacked early.

2. London System

The London System is a flexible and easy opening starting from the Queen's Pawn Opening. It starts with d4, Bishop to f4 and Knight to f3.The only disadvantage of this opening is the same as the Italian Opening.

3. French Defense

The French Defense a tactical opening with black where The King's Pawn goes to e6. It can lead to tactical position, or after the moves d4 and d5, It can turn into a simple exchange, It is a good opening for the black pieces.

4. Sicillian Defense

The Sicllian Defense is a opening the with Black Pieces with response of e4 with c5. It is a very aggresive opening that can lead to very positional and great games.
